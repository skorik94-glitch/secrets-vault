How it works

Scan. Vault. Safe access.

Three steps from “my keys are everywhere” to “my agent works with them and never sees a value.”

01

Scan

One read-only command inventories every credential across your machine and audits leaks — committed to git, world-readable, the same token reused across projects.

02

Vault

Bring them into one place — your own Infisical or an encrypted local store — with per-service runbooks the agent can read.

03

Safe access

Your AI agent gets references and instructions, never raw keys. Need a real value? A Touch ID prompt — then it's written to an audit log.

FAQ

The questions you should be asking.

Do my secrets get sent anywhere?

No. Everything runs locally — no network calls, no telemetry. The scan report contains paths, types and non-reversible fingerprints, never values.

What exactly does it read?

Files under the roots you choose (default: your home directory), and — optionally — browser history to discover which services you use. It never reads saved passwords or cookies.

How is it different from 1Password / Vault / Doppler?

Those are excellent vaults, but cloud-first and not AI-agent-native. Hush is local-first, reference-only for agents, with a built-in leak scanner and per-service runbooks.

Is it really free?

The full tool is MIT and free, forever. An optional, zero-knowledge cloud for cross-device and teams may come later — the local tool is never crippled to upsell it.
